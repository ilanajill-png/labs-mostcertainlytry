The Dialtones Europe Tour Buildout

Five Fundable Route Options

European Tour? Let's Go!

Prepared May 28, 2026. All hotels, media targets, and station contacts should be verified before booking or outreach.

Executive Recommendation

Most fundable right now

Option 2: Netherlands / Belgium / Germany is the cleanest operational route: short train hops, strong indie infrastructure, and fewer expensive crossings.

Best story route

Option 1: UK / Ireland / Netherlands is the easiest for the band to explain to supporters and U.S. grant reviewers.

Best visual route

Option 5: Italy / Germany / Austria gives the strongest music-video world, but it needs tighter logistics and likely local promoter help.

Funding thesis

The fundable version is not “we want to tour Europe.” It is a presenter-backed route with written venue interest, a clear budget, co-bill partners, and a cultural export narrative around Austin/Central Texas art-rock and recovery/resilience storytelling.

Option 1

UK / Ireland / Netherlands

Map base: Wikimedia Commons public-domain Europe map.

Touring-manager read

Best “first Europe” narrative: English-language markets plus Dutch indie infrastructure.

Travel mode

Plane/ferry for UK-Ireland-Netherlands jumps; train inside the Netherlands. A bus only makes sense if adding more UK/Ireland dates before crossing to Amsterdam.

Potential city order

London → Dublin → Cork → Amsterdam → Utrecht → Groningen

Open this route shortlist / CSV

Option 1

Pros / Cons

Pros

  • Strong language/cultural fit for an Austin band.
  • Dublin/London/Amsterdam have media ecosystems that understand indie folk-rock.
  • Easy to build a compact proof-of-concept tour around 6 cities.

Cons / risks

  • Water crossings make ground routing less elegant.
  • London and Amsterdam lodging can be expensive.
  • UK/Ireland/NL may require separate tax/admin handling.

Option 2

Netherlands / Belgium / Germany

Map base: Wikimedia Commons public-domain Europe map.

Touring-manager read

Best rail-first route: dense cities, strong indie venues, and easy Schengen movement.

Travel mode

Train-first. Add a rented van only if carrying full backline/merch. Flights are unnecessary unless dates are badly spaced.

Potential city order

Amsterdam → Utrecht → Groningen → Brussels → Antwerp → Cologne → Berlin → Hamburg → Stuttgart

Option 2

Pros / Cons

Pros

  • Very efficient geography.
  • Germany/Benelux have strong club infrastructure.
  • Multiple cities can be done with short travel days.

Cons / risks

  • Less obvious story hook than UK/Ireland.
  • Requires German/Dutch/Flemish local promoter knowledge.
  • Some acts/venues may expect European agent support.

  1. Pick one preferred route and one backup route.

  5. Choose whether to pursue local promoter help before direct venue outreach.

Next Manager Actions

  1. Verify the top 15 venues in the selected route: buyer route, room size, hold policy, backline, hospitality, and likely deal.
  2. Verify 10 co-bill artist teams and ask for local support recommendations.
  3. Create a date-hold grid with first/second-choice dates per city.
  4. Request presenter letters once there is real venue interest.
  5. Convert the budget into a grant-ready sheet: flights/trains, lodging, per diem, visas/work permits, backline, insurance, PR, and contingency.
