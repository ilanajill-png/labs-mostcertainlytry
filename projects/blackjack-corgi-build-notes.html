Project snapshot

The project pairs a Texas corgi playing-card deck with a browser blackjack card picker. The deck supplies 52 SVG faces, a matching card back, and a manifest; the picker uses those assets to show dealer and player cards while returning a basic-strategy recommendation.

Audience and reviewer

The immediate audience is a casual blackjack learner who wants fast hand lookup without a casino account. A secondary reviewer is someone evaluating the Labs site as proof of applied product thinking, static-site delivery, and visual iteration under feedback.

Problem being solved

Most blackjack calculators are plain, casino-adjacent, or too dense for casual practice. This build tests whether a themed, friendly, no-real-money interface can make basic strategy easier to approach while preserving the correct guardrails around gambling behavior.

Product or market lens

Benchmarking against Blackjack GTO, Blackjack Apprenticeship, Blackjack 3000, HitOrStand, BlackjackPilot, Wizard of Odds, and Learn Blackjack showed the current Labs tool is a good personality prototype, but not yet a top trainer. The gap is not theme; it is validation, scoring, rules coverage, and practice flow.

Timeline summary

The first pass shipped a working strategy picker and generated SVG deck. Follow-up review found that the deck looked too similar at thumbnail size, card thumbnails were clipping, and face cards were visually collapsing into literal 10 cards. The fix made the Texas cues bolder, used contained image rendering, and mapped J/Q/K to their own card art.

Key decisions

The tool defaults to a Vegas-style multi-deck setup with dealer standing on soft 17, double after split allowed, and toggles for H17 and surrender. The dealer upcard preselects to 6 so the page opens in a useful state. Asset URLs are versioned so GitHub Pages and browser caches do not hide fresh card art.

Tools and production method

The current deck is generated as static SVG with JavaScript and then consumed directly by plain HTML/CSS/JS pages. The next asset pass is planned around the OpenAI image-generation API guide: generate higher-quality corgi portrait art, place it into the card system, and publish with a new asset version.

Visual evidence

The deck preview page exposes every generated card for inspection. The picker page is the live integration test: it proves the card assets render in buttons, selected-hand chips, random practice hands, and dealer/player states without clipping or duplicate visual-card bugs.

Risks, gaps, and next steps

Remaining gaps: full source-of-truth validation against known strategy tables, training mode with grading, streak and weak-area tracking, shoe simulation, card counting mode, and stronger generated corgi portrait assets. The safety boundary remains explicit: the tool gives practice advice only and does not place bets or operate gambling accounts.
