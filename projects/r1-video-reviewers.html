Case Study

Building the Reviewer Panel for the R1 Video Project

A role-based critique system turned a short personal-agent promo into a stronger release candidate through focused reviewers, repeatable iteration, and platform QA.

Executive Summary

The R1 video project used a structured reviewer panel to turn a short personal-agent promo into a stronger, more publishable campaign asset. Instead of relying on one broad critique, the project created role-based reviewers with distinct jobs: awards craft, platform performance, business credibility, investor signal, partner usability, creator appeal, plain-language comprehension, accessibility, and audio identity.

The reviewer system became part of the production workflow itself. Each round exposed a different weakness: vague payoff, missing human proof, synthetic voice quality, generic audio identity, cropped screenshots, mobile safe-zone problems, and small workflow labels.

Reviewer Design

The first reviewer set included nine audience perspectives: Webby-style awards craft, TikTok performance, Instagram polish, hiring-company credibility, investor signal, partner usability, technical early-career appeal, sixth-grade comprehension, and retired-viewer accessibility.

Later rounds expanded the panel with motion/story craft and audio/music identity reviewers. That gave the project both expert critique and ordinary-viewer friction checks.

Iteration Pattern

  1. Build a candidate cut.
  2. Create or update a links page for the version.
  3. Ask the reviewer panel to evaluate the cut from assigned lenses.
  4. Summarize consensus and disagreements.
  5. Convert critique into concrete production changes.
  6. Re-render the next candidate.
  7. Run QA against platform exports and safe zones.

Final Outcome

V19 became the strongest release candidate. The larger tabs improved comprehension, the uncropped screenshot treatment fixed a trust problem, and the centered beige layout felt more intentional than earlier safe-zone passes.

Reusable Template

For future video projects, start with reviewers for awards craft, platform performance, business value, investor signal, partner workflow, early-career technical appeal, plain-language comprehension, accessibility, motion/story craft, and audio identity.
